\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

The study is small: \NumScoredEdits{} scored edits over \NumFacesScored{} faces,
with some model--procedure cells holding under ten samples and the chained
experiment probing a single face. We therefore report medians and ranges only and
draw no significance conclusions. The regional-change metric abstains on
non-frontal inputs, so the profile rhinoplasty faces contribute identity and
ground-truth scores but no localization evidence (one extreme profile defeats
face detection entirely)---precisely the poses where rhinoplasty previews matter
most clinically. The demographic
composition of the test set was not recorded, so nothing here speaks to performance
across skin tones, ages, or sexes; commercial face pipelines have documented gaps
of exactly that kind~\cite{buolamwini2018gendershades}. The expert rubric shipped
with the protocol has no collected scores yet, so clinical plausibility rests on
the automated metrics and the reader's own eyes. Finally, the models under test
are hosted services that change without notice; the pinned endpoints and run dates
make the study auditable but not permanently reproducible.
