\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Off-the-shelf hosted image editors cannot yet be trusted to confine a surgical
edit on their own---prompt-only control leaks half the change off-target for
every one of six editors, and the mask-accepting endpoint is the weakest rung,
not the strongest---but they do not need to be. A landmark-derived mask, an
alignment step, and a feathered composite on the client turn the same endpoints
into region-confined preview editors with localization near 1, at a median cost
of \$\MedCost{} per image. Model choice then becomes a legible trade between
edit strength and identity: FLUX.2 edits hardest and breaks the identity floor,
the Nano Banana models preserve identity but barely operate, and GPT Image~2 and
Seedream sit usably between. That framing, not any single model, is the finding:
the capability gap is in control, and control can live outside the model. The gating next step is clinical rather than technical---collecting
surgeon scores under the released rubric---alongside inpaint-capable endpoints
that actually edit, pose-robust masking for profile views, and a demographically
stratified test set.
